What's the difference between Celsius, Fahrenheit, and Kelvin?
+
Celsius (°C) is used in most countries, Fahrenheit (°F) primarily in the US, and Kelvin (K) in science. This tool converts instantly between all three temperature scales.
Why do I need to convert units?
+
Different countries use different measurement systems. The US uses imperial (pounds, miles, Fahrenheit) while most of the world uses metric (kilograms, kilometers, Celsius). Conversion is essential for international communication.
Can I convert between miles and kilometers?

Yes! Select "Distance" converter, enter your value in miles, and select kilometers. The tool converts instantly. 1 mile = 1.60934 kilometers.

What's the metric system?
+
The metric system is an international standard of measurement using base-10 (decimals). It includes meters (distance), kilograms (weight), liters (volume), and Celsius (temperature).
What's the imperial system?
+
The imperial system, primarily used in the United States, includes inches/feet/miles (distance), pounds/ounces (weight), gallons/cups (volume), and Fahrenheit (temperature).
How many ounces in a pound?
+
There are 16 ounces in 1 pound. Use the Weight converter to easily convert between pounds and ounces or switch to metric (kilograms, grams).
What's the freezing point of water?
+
Water freezes at 0°C (32°F or 273.15K). Use the Temperature converter to understand reference points between scales.
Can I convert absolute zero?
+
Yes! Absolute zero is 0K (-273.15°C or -459.67°F). The tool handles extreme temperatures and converts between all three scales accurately.
How do I convert body temperature?
+
Normal body temperature is 37°C (98.6°F). Use the Temperature converter to understand fever temperatures or convert health readings between scales.
Can I use this for homework and studies?

Absolutely! This tool is perfect for verifying homework conversions, studying unit conversion concepts, and understanding different measurement systems in science and math classes.
