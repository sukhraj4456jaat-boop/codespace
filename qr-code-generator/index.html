Frequently Asked Questions

What is a QR code?
+
QR (Quick Response) code is a 2D barcode that stores information readable by smartphones and QR scanners. It can encode URLs, text, emails, phone numbers, and more—all in a scannable matrix pattern.
What can I encode in a QR code?
+
URLs, plain text, emails, phone numbers, WiFi credentials, vCards (contact info), calendar events, SMS messages, and more. You can encode up to 4,296 characters depending on content type.
What is error correction?
+
Error correction allows QR codes to work even if partially damaged or obscured. There are 4 levels: Low (7% recovery), Medium (15%), Quartile (25%), High (30%). Use High for outdoor/printed codes.
Should I use PNG or SVG format?
+
Use PNG for web images and digital display. Use SVG for printing since it scales infinitely without quality loss, maintaining crisp edges at any size.
How do I customize the colors?
+
Use the color pickers to select "Dark Color" (the QR pattern) and "Light Color" (the background). Any RGB color works, but ensure sufficient contrast for scanning.
What size should my QR code be?
+
Minimum recommended size is 200x200 pixels for digital use. For printing, larger is better: 500x500+ pixels ensures reliable scanning from distance. Use the size slider to adjust.
Is this tool secure and private?
+
100% secure and private. All QR code generation happens in your browser. No data is sent to servers, and nothing is logged or tracked. Your data stays on your device.
Can I use this offline?
+
Yes! Once the page loads, you can generate QR codes completely offline. All processing happens in your browser, requiring no internet connection.
How do I download the QR code?
+
After generating the QR code, click "Download PNG" for web use or "Download SVG" for printing. The file downloads to your device's default downloads folder.
What are the limitations?
+
QR codes can store up to 4,296 characters for URLs/text. Very long URLs require higher error correction levels or creating a shortened URL first. Extremely complex designs may reduce scannability.
Can I use QR codes for business?
+
Absolutely! Generate unlimited commercial-quality QR codes for business cards, packaging, marketing, menus, tickets, and more. No licensing restrictions or usage limitations.
How do QR code scanners work?
+
Most smartphone cameras have built-in QR scanning (iOS 11+, Android 8+). Simply point your camera at the code and tap the notification that appears. Or use dedicated QR scanner apps.
Can I edit a QR code after creating it?
+
QR codes are static and immutable once generated. To change the content, create a new code. For dynamic URLs (change destination without new code), use URL shorteners with analytics.
What if my QR code doesn't scan?
+
Possible causes: insufficient contrast between colors, very small size, poor quality image, or outdated scanner. Try: increasing size, using high/medium error correction, ensuring good color contrast, or testing with multiple scanners.
Can I track QR code scans?
+
This tool doesn't track scans. For tracking, use URL shorteners (bit.ly, TinyURL) that provide analytics, or QR code services with tracking features like analytics dashboards.
What's the best use case for QR codes?
+
QR codes excel at: bridging physical and digital (print material to website), contactless information sharing, payment processing, event ticketing, product authentication, and WiFi sharing without typing passwords.

More Information & Tips

  • Test Before Printing: Always test your QR code with multiple devices before printing large batches to ensure scannability.
  • Maintain Contrast: Never use similar colors for dark/light (e.g., dark blue on black). Ensure clear contrast for reliable scanning.
  • Add White Border: Include a white quiet zone (margin) around the QR code to improve scanning reliability.
  • High Error Correction for Outdoor: Use High (30%) error correction for outdoor/outdoor signs where codes may get dirty or damaged.
  • Use Shortened URLs: For complex URLs, use shorteners (bit.ly, tinyurl) to keep QR codes simpler and more scannable.
  • Test on Mobile: Always scan with actual mobile phones (not just emulators) before deployment to ensure real-world performance.
  • Mobile-Friendly Landing Pages: Direct QR codes to mobile-optimized pages for best user experience on smartphones.
  • Include Call-to-Action: Next to QR codes, add text like "Scan for more info" to encourage scanning.
  • Track with Analytics: Use URL shorteners or analytics platforms to track QR code performance and scan locations.
  • Avoid Excessive Branding: While you can customize colors, avoid designs that reduce scannability. Test extensively before final designs.
